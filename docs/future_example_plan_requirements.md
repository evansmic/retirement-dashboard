# Future Example Plan Requirements

## Purpose

Define the fresh example set for the future clean saved format.

## Required Examples

### Single Person With A Covered Floor

Purpose: show the basic capacity-first answer without couple or survivor complexity.

Must include:

- minimum monthly expenses,
- monthly after-tax capacity,
- spending-path defaults.

Must avoid:

- migrated desired-spending values,
- account withdrawal instructions.

### Couple With A Tight Or Uncovered Floor

Purpose: test gap language and practical options without pushing one answer.

Must include:

- minimum expense gap,
- work-longer comparison,
- downsize comparison,
- tax review note.

Must avoid:

- advice-like instructions,
- false certainty that one option fixes the plan.

### DB Pension Couple With Survivor Sensitivity

Purpose: keep pension continuation and survivor resilience visible.

Must include:

- DB pension continuation,
- survivor review,
- after-tax monthly answer.

Must avoid:

- hiding survivor impact behind a single household number.

### Estate-Heavy Plan With Room Above The Floor

Purpose: test discretionary-room wording without sounding like permission to spend more.

Must include:

- estate intent,
- room above floor,
- tax and spending-path caveats.

Must avoid:

- safe-spend language,
- guaranteed-room language.

## Boundary

These are requirements only. No current examples are changed in this package.
